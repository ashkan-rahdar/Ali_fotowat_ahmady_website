About

Ali Fotowat-Ahmady was born in Tehran, Iran, in 1958. He received the B.S. degree from the California Institute of Technology, Pasadena, CA, USA, in 1980, and the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in electrical engineering from Stanford University, Stanford, CA, USA, in 1982 and 1991, respectively. He started his career at Philips Semiconductor in Sunnyvale, CA, USA, in 1987 where he developed several integrated circuits for mobile phones. In 1991 he joined the Electrical Engineering Department of Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran. His research interests include advanced integrated circuits for energy savings and communication/positioning applications.

Associate Professor of EE at Sharif

Seeking system and custom IC design consulting projects in the areas of 4G-LTE, IoT and M2M, DSRC, GNSS, Wearable electronics and Automotive radar. In the past 23 years in our consulting company we have finished and delivered more than 70 such projects.

Due to his interests in entrepreneurial engineering, he has been the co-founder of several companies and continues advising his students on the same. Dr. Fotowat-Ahmady is a three times recipient of the Kharazmi Science and Engineering Award for his work on low-power microelectronics and communication ICs. He is a member of the IEEE Solid-State Society and has been the adviser of the society's Sharif Electrical Engineering student chapter. He is also a member of the executive committee of the Karaya Angel Fund (An NGO promoting new start-ups).

Activities and societies: IEEE member since 1978

I was a Professor James D. Meindl student. I developed micropower systems and chips for implantable ultrasonic blood flow meters. These included integrated analog transcoductor-C filters with nA level currents, plus a low power FM transmitter. I also did some work on implantable antennas. I was probably one of the last PhD students in the world "fabricating" my own chip.
